MOTOR VEHICLES ACT, 1988

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

Section 1. Short title and extent.
This Act may be called the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. It extends to the whole of India.

Section 2. Definitions.
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:
- "Motor vehicle" means any vehicle which is propelled by an internal combustion engine or electric motor;
- "License" means a driving license issued under Chapter IV of this Act;
- "Owner" means any person in whose name a motor vehicle stands registered;
- "Transport vehicle" means a motor vehicle constructed or adapted to carry, or to carry and draw, goods or passengers.

CHAPTER IV — DRIVING LICENSES

Section 3. Requirement of license for driving motor vehicles.
No person shall drive a motor vehicle in any public place unless he holds an effective driving license issued in his name authorizing him to drive motor vehicles of that class or description at that time.
PENALTY: Violation is a criminal offense under Section 180 of this Act.

Section 4. Application for driving license.
Every application for a driving license shall be made in such form and shall contain such particulars and be accompanied by such evidence as may be prescribed.
REQUIREMENTS: Valid identity proof, address proof, learner's license (if applicable), medical certificate for vehicles over 8 years old.

Section 5. Learner's license.
A person desiring to learn to drive a motor vehicle may apply to the authority for a learner's license. Such person shall not be less than 16 years of age for two wheelers or 18 years for four wheelers.
VALIDITY: Learner's license is valid for 6 months from the date of issue.

Section 6. Issue of Driving License.
The authority shall issue a driving license in such form as may be prescribed, to an applicant who has passed the driving test and satisfied all other requirements.
VALIDITY: License valid for 10 years, can be renewed for another 10 years.

Section 180. Driving without license.
Whoever drives a motor vehicle on any public place without a valid driving license shall be guilty of an offense.
PUNISHMENT: 
- First offense: Imprisonment up to 6 months OR fine up to Rs. 500 OR both
- Subsequent offense within 3 years: Imprisonment up to 3 months AND fine of Rs. 1,000

Section 181. Driving without learner's license.
Any person who drives a motor vehicle without a valid learner's license when required shall be punished with fine up to Rs. 300 for first offense and up to Rs. 500 for subsequent offense within 12 months.

Section 182. Violation of conditions of license.
Whoever drives a motor vehicle in violation of any condition for the time being attached to his driving license shall be guilty of an offense and shall on conviction be liable to a fine which may extend to Rs. 500 for the first offense and Rs. 1,000 for any subsequent offense.

Section 183. Rash or negligent driving.
Whoever drives any motor vehicle in a rash or negligent manner so as to endanger human life or likely to cause injury to any person or property shall on conviction be liable:
- Imprisonment up to 6 months OR fine up to Rs. 1,000 OR both
- For second offense within 12 months: Imprisonment up to 3 months AND fine of Rs. 2,000 with possibility of license suspension.

Section 184. Causing death by negligence.
Whoever causes death of any person by driving a motor vehicle in a rash or negligent manner shall be:
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 2 years OR fine up to Rs. 10,000 OR both, and motor vehicle may be forfeited.
CONSEQUENCES: Driving license cancellation/suspension, must reapply after 6 months to 1 year.

Section 185. Driving under influence of alcohol or drugs.
Whoever drives or attempts to drive a motor vehicle, having consumed alcohol or drugs in such quantity as to be incapable of exercising effective control over the vehicle shall be:
PUNISHMENT: 
- First offense: Imprisonment up to 6 months OR fine up to Rs. 2,000 OR both
- Second offense within 3 years: Imprisonment up to 2 years AND fine of Rs. 3,000
CONSEQUENCES: License suspension for 6 months to 1 year, mandatory rehabilitation counseling, vehicle may be impounded.

Section 186. Use of mobile phone while driving.
Whoever drives a motor vehicle while using a mobile phone or communicating device (not hands-free) shall be:
PUNISHMENT: Fine of Rs. 1,000 for first offense, Rs. 5,000 for second offense within 12 months.
CONSEQUENCES: No imprisonment for this offense, but repeated violations may affect license renewal.

Section 187. Seat belt and safety measures.
Every person driving or sitting in a motor vehicle shall wear a safety belt as prescribed.
PUNISHMENT: Fine of Rs. 1,000 for driver, Rs. 100 for passenger for first offense.
CONSEQUENCES: No imprisonment, but added fine for repeat offenses.

Section 188. Helmet requirement for two-wheeler riders.
The driver of a two-wheeled motor vehicle and the passenger shall wear protective helmets of approved standard while driving or riding.
PUNISHMENT: Fine of Rs. 1,000 for first offense, Rs. 2,000 for second offense within 12 months.
CONSEQUENCES: Vehicle may be detained, license endorsement warning.

Section 189. Traffic violations and penalties.
Traffic violations including jumping traffic signals, wrong-way driving, speeding, etc.
COMMON PENALTIES:
- Signal jumping: Rs. 1,000 fine
- Speeding: Rs. 400-2,000 depending on excess speed
- Illegal parking: Rs. 500-2,000
- Wrong side driving: Rs. 500-1,000
CONSEQUENCES: License points deduction, suspension if 12+ points in violation.

Section 190. Driving without proper documents.
Driving without vehicle registration certificate or insurance certificate.
PUNISHMENT: Fine up to Rs. 500 for driving without registration, Rs. 2,000 for driving without insurance.
CONSEQUENCES: Vehicle may be seized and auctioned, driving license suspension possible.

Section 191. Vehicle fitness certificate.
Every motor vehicle engaged in transport or used commercially shall obtain a fitness certificate from Registered Vehicle Inspector (RVI) yearly.
PUNISHMENT: Driving without fitness certificate: Fine up to Rs. 1,000 for first offense, Rs. 2,000 for subsequent offenses.
CONSEQUENCES: Vehicle impoundment, operation license cancellation.

Section 192. Invalid or fake license.
Whoever drives using a forged, fake, or suspended driving license.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 years AND fine up to Rs. 10,000.
CONSEQUENCES: Permanent license cancellation, criminal record, vehicle seizure.

Section 193. Unauthorized operation of vehicle.
Driving someone else's motor vehicle without the owner's permission or valid authority.
PUNISHMENT: Imprisonment up to 3 months OR fine up to Rs. 1,000 OR both.
CONSEQUENCES: Criminal record, vehicle seizure, license demerit points.

CHAPTER VII — INSURANCE

Section 194. Third party insurance mandatory.
Every motor vehicle shall be covered by a valid Third Party (TP) insurance policy before operating on public roads.
CONSEQUENCES: Driving without insurance: Fine up to Rs. 2,000 for first offense, Rs. 4,000 + vehicle impoundment for subsequent offenses.

PENALTIES FOR LICENSE-RELATED ISSUES:

Issue: Forgot to carry license while driving
LEGAL CONSEQUENCE: Can be fined Rs. 500 (not imprisonment), but must produce license at police station within 7 days to avoid higher fine.
ADVICE: Always carry original license copy.

Issue: License suspended
CONSEQUENCE: Driving during suspension: Fine Rs. 1,000-5,000, imprisonment up to 3 months, license cancellation.

Issue: Expired license
CONSEQUENCE: Same as driving without license - Rs. 500 fine, must renew immediately.

Issue: License revocation
CONSEQUENCE: Cannot drive any motor vehicle until reapplying after 1-3 years, new test required.

Issue: Multiple violations (12+ points)
CONSEQUENCE: License automatic suspension for 3 months, must attend training course to reactivate.

DRIVING LICENSE CATEGORIZATION:

LMV-M (Light Motor Vehicle-Manual): Manual transmission 2 wheelers - Age 16+
LMV-AT (Light Motor Vehicle-Automatic): Automatic 2 wheelers - Age 16+
MC (Motorcycle): Bikes above 50cc - Age 18+
MCWG (Motorcycle with Gear): Heavy bikes - Age 18+
CAR (Cars): 4 wheelers - Age 18+
TAXI: Commercial vehicles - Age 18+, Taxi badge required
HMV (Heavy Motor Vehicle): Commercial trucks - Age 18+, Professional qualification needed
PSV (Passenger Service Vehicle): Buses - Age 18+, Professional qualification needed
